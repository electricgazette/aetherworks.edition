Britannia Rules the Skies?

Hydrogen “Cloud-Ship” Tested Above Portsmouth

A strange spectacle was observed yesterday above the Royal Dockyard, Portsmouth, when Captain Erasmus Lanyon, late of the Royal Engineers, caused to be launched his experimental Aerial Monitoring Balloon, or “Cloud-Ship.”

Suspended beneath a modest envelope of hydrogen and propelled by a paddling apparatus reminiscent of a duck in distress, the craft succeeded in circling the harbour twice before alighting in a brewer’s yard.

While certain naval authorities expressed doubt as to the martial value of a vessel that could be punctured by an indignant seagull, it was agreed that the overhead view of the anchorage was “most instructive.”

A proposal that the Cloud-Ship be fitted with a small brass cannon was referred to a committee.
