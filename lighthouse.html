The Whispering Lighthouse of Orkney

Coastguard Hears the Fog Speak in Greek

Kirkwall, Orkney

At the remote lighthouse of Grim Ness there stands a new lantern recently fitted with what the Board of Trade describes as an Electro-Phonic Signalling Apparatus. By the mingling of electricity with compressed air, this device produces a sound which, to a sober mariner, resembles a melancholy cow.

Last Thursday night, however, the keeper, Mr. Angus Macrimmon, swore before witnesses that the fog-horn distinctly uttered the words “Phi – Psi – Omega”. Being unversed in foreign alphabets, he telegraphed the anomaly to Edinburgh, where a Professor of Greek declared the sounds to be “a creditable rendering of the last letters of that venerable tongue.”

Whether this constitutes a warning from the depths, from the heavens, or from some prematurely fatigued pressure-valve remains under investigation. The Board of Trade has ordered that, until further notice, all notes in the range of classical declension be avoided.
