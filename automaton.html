An Automaton Clerk for the Counting-House

Machine Promises to Total Ledgers & Terrify Junior Staff

City of London, Monday

Messrs. Peabody & Kincaid, Accountants of Bishopsgate, have installed in their offices an apparatus known as the Arithmetical Clerk-Engine, constructed under the supervision of Dr. Leverson. The machine, consisting of a nest of brass wheels, numbered drums, and a lever much resembling a pump-handle, is said to compute sums of fearful magnitude without once resorting to the back of an envelope.

A junior clerk, upon being invited to enter a column of figures, was observed to grow paler with each clang of the mechanism. “If it can reckon the interest,” he murmured, “what further use have they for me?”

Investors need not fear; the inventors assure us that the engine has no capacity to go to lunch, and therefore can never replace human labour entirely.
